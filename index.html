Who is Andrew Vella?

Andrew Vella is a Graduate of Skidmore College with a Bachelor’s in Computer Science and a minor in Religious Studies with a lifelong passion for technology education. He has taught computer programming, algebra, and theater to children from ages five to fifteen. Additionally, He has volunteered for Goddard Riverside, tutoring in reading comprehension and writing at the Star Learning Center.

Andrew developed an interest in computer science as a child, developing video games in the Scratch programming language. He experimented with game engines like Processing, Godot, and Unity, before learning Python for data science and starting his degree with some Java programming. He further experimented with developing his own toy programming languages, as a study in the history of computer programming. Most recently, he has developed an introductory course in Python programming and Turtle graphics dubbed "Code Redstart" after the American Redstart, a species of migratory bird.

Andrew believes that understanding modern computers is only growing more necessary, as electronics command more of our time and attention. A computer can be very useful, but it cannot be your friend. The computer operates for whoever programs it, without loyalty. Today, children are using computers more than ever, and they deserve to know how the glowing boxes actually work, so they can take ownership of their digital lives. The purpose of Code Redstart is to help kids use technology, rather than letting technology use them.

When the weather is nice, Andrew can be found photographing birds and other wildlife, which he shares to Instagram and Flickr. Turtles, flowers, and other more cooperative subjects are also on the list of photographic features. You can also find him at the climbing gym, on rock walls, belaying friends, and occasionally scaling outdoor crags.
